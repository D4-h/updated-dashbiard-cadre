Project Overview

Public Service Recruitment Reform in Maharashtra

This project proposes a major reform of Maharashtra‚Äôs recruitment framework, covering 2,500 cadres across 33 departments. The current system is fragmented, with over 2000+ separate exams and inconsistent, outdated rules for identical posts.

Our solution introduces a cadre bucketing framework to group similar roles and standardize requirements. The approach focuses on:

  • Uniform rules for identical cadres across departments.
  • Uniform rules for identical cadres across departments.
  • Digitization of all rules in a single repository.

    • By consolidating thousands of exams into a few qualification-based tests, the initiative will deliver a transparent, efficient system that reduces administrative burden and ensures fair, merit-based selection.

Strategy Towards Reform

Resource Structure

The pyramid illustrates the distribution of these cadres across hierarchical groups, with Group C forming the largest base.

Exam Schemes

This complexity results in a multitude of exams, with over 2500 different recruitment tests being conducted for various cadres.

Indentified Issues

Critical flaws identified in the existing recruitment rules that compromise the quality and fairness of hiring.

Illegible Physical Records

The reliance on poorly maintained and often illegible physical copies of recruitment rules. This makes it extremely difficult to accurately access, interpret, and digitize the governing criteria for cadres

Preferences in Eligibility

Subjective clauses, such as preferring candidates with post-graduate degrees or published papers, complicate the selection process and can lead to inconsistencies.

Restrictive Institute Recognition

Recruitment rules recognize qualifications from only one specific institute, limiting the pool of eligible candidates.

Solution: Three Proposals for RR Revamp

The proposed strategy involves ensuring uniformity for the same cadres, standardizing all RRs using a model template, and digitizing them into a single, accessible repository.

Cadre Bucketing - First Level

The strategic framework of "bucketing" to streamline and rationalize the ~2500 cadres. The first level of this framework involves a broad functional classification of all cadres into three primary categories: Administrative, Technical, and Developmental.

Cadre Bucketing - Administrative Domain

Bucketing for Administrative cadres, breaking them down into more specific domains. These sub-categories include General, Legal, Finance, Law Enforcement, and Other Admin to better group similar roles.

Cadre Bucketing - Technical Domain

Bucketing for Technical cadres, organizing them based on their area of expertise. The primary sub-groups include Engineering, Health, Sciences, and Mathematics/Statistical, among others.

Cadre Bucketing - Developmental Domain

Bucketing for Developmental cadres, which are grouped based on their functional roles in societal development. The key domains identified are Education and Social Welfare, with a third category for other developmental roles.

Guiding Principles for Bucketing

The four principal lenses, or criteria, that guide the cadre bucketing and recruitment rule analysis. The approach focuses on re-evaluating the relevance and verifiability of requirements related to Education, Experience, Skills, and Physical Qualifications

Proposed Exam Scheme for Group A and B (Gazetted)

A proposal to consolidate recruitment for Group A and B (Gazetted) posts into two primary examination schemes. The approach suggests combining exams based on educational criteria, such as a unified Maharashtra Civil Service exam and a separate stream-based CBT for senior officer levels.

Proposed Exam Scheme for Group B (Non-Gazetted) and C

A proposal of simplified two-exam scheme for all Group B (Non-Gazetted) and Group C recruitment. The model suggests a Combined Non-Gazetted exam for graduates/diploma holders and a single Maharashtra Staff Selection exam for HSC/SSC qualified candidates, with no interviews for these posts.
